Project Overview

Led a procurement and supply chain transformation using the Lean Six Sigma DMAIC methodology. Identified and eliminated 6 critical bottlenecks, renegotiated contracts, and consolidated suppliers to drive operational excellence.

DMAIC Framework Applied

  • Define: Problem identified – procurement team spending 40 hours/week on manual tasks with frequent supplier delays.
  • Measure: Baseline metrics established (labor hours, supplier on-time delivery %, cycle times).
  • Analyze: Used cause-and-effect analysis and process mapping to identify 6 bottlenecks.
  • Improve: Renegotiated contracts, consolidated suppliers, introduced automated reorder triggers.
  • Control: Implemented new SOPs, weekly performance reviews, and real-time Power BI dashboards.

Measurable Impact

  • Labor Efficiency: Cut weekly procurement labor from 40 hours to 14 hours (65% reduction).
  • Supplier Performance: Raised on-time delivery from 65% to 98%.
  • Cycle Time: Reduced operational cycle time by 35%.
  • Cost Savings: Delivered $18,000 annual savings through reduced emergency procurement.

Tools & Methodologies

Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC) · Process Mapping · Cause-and-Effect Analysis · Supplier Consolidation · Contract Negotiation · SOP Development
